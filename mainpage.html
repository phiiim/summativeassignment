ART MUSEUM GALLERY





Édouard Manet
23 January 1832 – 30 April 1883

He was a French modernist painter.
He was one of the first 19th-century artists to paint modern life, as well as a pivotal figure in the transition from Realism to Impressionism.

Vincent Willem van Gogh
30 March 1853 – 29 July 1890

He was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter who posthumously became one of the most famous and influential figures in Western art history

Diego Velázquez
June 6, 1599 – August 6, 1660

He was a Spanish painter, the leading artist in the court of King Philip IV of Spain and Portugal, and of the Spanish Golden Age.
Velázquez's paintings became a model for 19th-century realist and impressionist painters.

Joseph Mallord William Turner
23 April 1775 – 19 December 1851

He was an English Romantic painter, printmaker and watercolourist.
He is known for his expressive colouring, imaginative landscapes and turbulent, often violent marine paintings.

Pissarro Camille
10 July 1830 – 13 November 1903

He was a Danish-French Impressionist and Neo-Impressionist painter born on the island of St Thomas.

Joan Miró
20 April 1893 – 25 December 1983

He was a Spanish painter, sculptor and ceramicist born in Barcelona.
